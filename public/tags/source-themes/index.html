Dynamic macro scale traffic flow optimisation using crowd-sourced urban movement data

Urban movement data as collected by location-based social networks provides valuable information about routes and specific roads that people are likely to drive on. This allows us to pinpoint roads that occur in many routes and are thus sensitive to congestion. Redistributing some of the traffic to avoid unnecessary use of these roads could be a key factor in improving traffic flow. Many of the previously proposed approaches to combat congestion are either static (e.g. a city tax) or do not incorporate any movement data and hence ignore how citizens use the infrastructure. In this work, we present a method to redistribute traffic through the introduction of externally imposed variable costs to each road segment, assuming that all drivers seek to drive the cheapest route.

Examination of the genetic factors underlying the cognitive variability associated with neurofibromatosis type 1

We investigated cognitive variability and assessed thecontribution of genetic factors by performing a retrospective cohortstudy and a monozygotic twin case series. We included data of 497children with genetically confirmed NF1 and an IQ assessment,including 12 monozygotic twin and 17 sibling sets.
